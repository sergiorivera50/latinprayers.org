# The Tridentine Mass (1962 Missale Romanum): An Algorithmic Order of Service

## Edition Note / Scope
This document describes the Mass according to the **1962 Missale Romanum** (the Missal of John XXIII), the edition authorized by *Summorum Pontificum* (2007) and used by the FSSP, ICKSP, and most traditional communities. It embodies the **new Code of Rubrics** that John XXIII commanded in his motu proprio *Rubricarum instructum* (25 July 1960) — "beginning on the first day of January … 1961, all those who follow the Roman rite shall observe the new code of rubrics of the Roman breviary and missal" — promulgated by the Sacred Congregation of Rites' decree *Novum rubricarum* (26 July 1960), with the Missal rubrics numbered nn. 269–530.

Where 1962 differs from earlier editions, this is flagged. Notable 1962 features vs. pre-1955/pre-1960 usage:
- The pre-Communion Confiteor of the servers/deacon is **omitted** (it was said before 1962; it survives in the 1962 Pontificale and on Good Friday, and was "omitted, not expressly forbidden").
- **Benedicamus Domino** is now restricted to far fewer cases; **Ite Missa est** is used in most instances where Benedicamus was formerly said.
- Incense was made permissible at **every** Missa Cantata: Code of Rubrics no. 426 (1960) — *"Incensationes quae in Missa solemni fieri debent, fieri possunt etiam in omnibus Missis cantatis"* ("The incensations that are obligatory in Solemn Mass are permitted in every Missa Cantata").
- The pre-1955 Holy Week and the older octaves/vigils differ substantially but are outside this scope.

A foundational principle (Adrian Fortescue, *Catholic Encyclopedia*, 1910, "Liturgy of the Mass"): **Solemn Mass is the norm; Low Mass is its simplification** — "the essence of high Mass is not the music but the deacon and subdeacon." At Solemn Mass the celebrant himself quietly reads even the parts sung by deacon, subdeacon, or choir. The 1962 Code of Rubrics no. 271 formally classified "a sung Mass, when celebrated without sacred ministers" as a *Missa cantata*.

## The Three Axes of Variation
1. **Form / solemnity:** Low Mass (Missa lecta/privata) → Missa Cantata (sung, no deacon/subdeacon) → Solemn High Mass (Missa solemnis, with deacon + subdeacon).
2. **Liturgical day & season:** rank/class of the day (I–IV class), season, and feast vs. feria determine the color, whether Gloria and Credo are said, what falls between Epistle and Gospel, the dismissal form, commemorations, and whether it is a Requiem.
3. **Preliminary rites:** Asperges/Vidi Aquam before the principal Sunday Mass.

## AXIS 1 — Forms of the Mass

**Low Mass (Missa lecta):** Priest + one or two servers. Everything spoken (parts silent or audible per rubric); no chant, no incense normally. 2 altar candles (a 3rd from Consecration to Communion per the Missal rubric; 4 if celebrated by a bishop). Server(s) make the Latin responses for the people. Followed by the Leonine Prayers.

**Missa Cantata (sung Mass):** Priest + servers, no deacon/subdeacon. Ordinary and Propers sung by celebrant and choir; incense permitted (Code of Rubrics no. 426). 4 or 6 candles. The celebrant chants Collect, Epistle (may be read), Gospel, Preface, Pater Noster, Ite Missa Est; choir sings Introit, Kyrie, Gloria, Gradual/Alleluia/Tract, Credo, Offertory, Sanctus, Agnus Dei, Communion. Incense may be used as at Solemn Mass **except the celebrant is not incensed after the Gospel** (no deacon to do so, though many places do incense him). No Leonine Prayers.

**Solemn High Mass (Missa solemnis):** Celebrant + deacon + subdeacon + servers (MC, thurifer, acolytes, torchbearers). 6 candles (7 for Pontifical Mass by the Ordinary, the seventh behind the cross). Subdeacon chants the Epistle; deacon chants the Gospel. Full incensations and the Kiss of Peace (Pax). The roles of deacon/subdeacon are frequently filled by priests (or a cleric/"straw" subdeacon, who may do all the subdeacon does except handle the chalice/pour water). The celebrant reads quietly the texts being sung, then **sits at the sedilia** with the ministers while the choir finishes longer chants. No Leonine Prayers.

**Key shared feature of High/Solemn Mass — silent-while-sung:** The celebrant reads the Introit, Kyrie, Gloria, Gradual/Alleluia, Credo, Offertory, Sanctus, Agnus Dei, and Communion in a low voice at the altar. Having finished the Gloria and Credo (and during a long Kyrie or Gradual), he goes to the sedilia and sits until the choir finishes, then resumes. He does **not** sit for the Sanctus/Agnus Dei (his own action there usually outlasts the chant — and he must not interrupt the Canon). When he intones "Gloria in excelsis Deo" or "Credo in unum Deum," the choir continues from "Et in terra pax" / "Patrem omnipotentem" (the choir must not repeat the intoned words).

## AXIS 2 — Day & Season Branch Points (quick reference)

- **Liturgical color** (visible branch): **White** (Christmas/Easter seasons, feasts of the Lord not of the Passion, BVM, angels, non-martyr confessors/virgins, Trinity); **Red** (Pentecost, Apostles, Martyrs, Passion feasts); **Green** (Sundays/ferias per annum — Time after Epiphany & Pentecost); **Violet** (Advent, Septuagesima–Lent, vigils that are fast days, ember days, Rogations, penitential votives); **Black** (Requiems, Good Friday); **Rose** (Gaudete = 3rd Advent, Laetare = 4th Lent). Determined by the rank/season of the day.

- **Feast ranking (1962):** four classes — **I, II, III, IV class** (replacing the older Simple/Semidouble/Double system). All Sundays are I or II class. This ranking governs precedence, color, and whether Gloria/Credo are said.

- **Gloria** — SAID on Sundays except Advent/Septuagesima/Lent/Passiontide; on feasts III class or higher; on white-color ferias (Christmas–Jan 13, Easter–Pentecost); on votives III class+ (not if violet), the IV-class votive of the Angels, and of the BVM on Saturday. OMITTED whenever violet is worn, in Requiems, and in most IV-class Masses.

- **Credo** — SAID on all Sundays; on I-class feasts; on II-class feasts of the Lord, Our Lady, Apostles/Evangelists (plus Chair of St. Peter, St. Barnabas); in octaves of Christmas/Easter/Pentecost; on I-class votives. OMITTED on most other days (II-class not included above, III/IV class, Requiems). Mnemonic: the altar Missal prints "Credo" after the Gospel when it is to be said.

- **Between Epistle and Gospel:**
  - Most of the year: **Gradual + Alleluia** (with verse).
  - Septuagesima → Lent/Passiontide: **Gradual + Tract** (no Alleluia).
  - Paschaltide (Easter→Pentecost): **two Alleluias** (the great Alleluia replaces the Gradual), except the Easter octave keeps Gradual + Alleluia.
  - Advent ferias: Gradual only (Alleluia + verse only on the Sunday).
  - **Sequence** (sung after the Alleluia/Tract, before the Gospel) on only five occasions: *Victimae Paschali laudes* (Easter), *Veni Sancte Spiritus* (Pentecost), *Lauda Sion* (Corpus Christi), *Stabat Mater* (Seven Sorrows of the BVM — added 1727 by Benedict XIII), *Dies Irae* (Requiems; optional/*ad libitum* at the Daily Mass for the Dead).

- **Dismissal form:** **Ite Missa est** when the Gloria was said; **Benedicamus Domino** when the Gloria was omitted (1962 restricts this to far fewer cases than before); **Requiescant in pace** at Requiems (R. *Amen*, not *Deo gratias*). From the Easter Vigil to Low Sunday and on Pentecost, "alleluia, alleluia" is appended to both dismissal and response.

- **Commemorations:** Additional Collects (with corresponding Secrets and Postcommunions) of a concurring feast/feria/Sunday. 1962 caps the total at three with no exceptions (generally only one privileged commemoration on I-class or sung Masses). They slot in immediately after the day's own Collect (and correspondingly after the Secret and Postcommunion), per the *Tabula Orationum*.

- **Day of the week:** The weekday *name* by itself does **not** change the Ordinary. Variation is driven by the calendar — the rank/class of the day and the season — not by "Monday vs. Tuesday." However, ferias in penitential seasons (Advent, Lent, Passiontide) have specific features: no Gloria, often no Alleluia (Tract or Gradual-only), violet color, and on certain Lenten ferias an extra *Oratio super populum* with a prescribed genuflection. Votive Masses (e.g., First Friday Sacred Heart) are *chosen* on certain weekdays at the celebrant's option, not by an automatic weekday rule.

## AXIS 3 — Asperges / Vidi Aquam (before the principal Sunday Mass)

Performed immediately before the principal Mass on Sundays (it precedes the blessing of palms/candles when those occur). **Omitted** on Palm Sunday (replaced by blessing of palms) and when a Pontifical High Mass is celebrated; omitted (as to the people) when the Blessed Sacrament is exposed. The celebrant wears a **cope** of the day's color.

- **Asperges Me** (Ps. 50 / *Miserere*) — throughout the year. Structure: antiphon → psalm verse → Gloria Patri (omitted in Passiontide) → antiphon repeated.
- **Vidi Aquam** (Ezech. 47, with Alleluias) — replaces Asperges throughout **Paschaltide** (Easter→Pentecost).
- **Actor/action:** Celebrant kneels and intones the antiphon, then sprinkles the altar (center, Epistle side, Gospel side), then the ministers/servers, then processes through the church sprinkling the people; choir sings antiphon/psalm/Gloria Patri. Concludes with versicles ("Ostende nobis…") and a Collect. **Spoken aloud if no music; sung at sung Masses.** Congregation stands.

## MASTER ORDERED FLOW OF THE MASS

Notation for each step: **Actor** · **Physical action / position** · **Voice (silent/aloud; spoken/sung)** · **Congregation posture** · **Branch notes**. Positions: *center* = middle of altar; *Epistle side* = celebrant's-right/liturgical south; *Gospel side* = celebrant's-left/liturgical north. A **genuflection** is made before the altar when the Blessed Sacrament is in the tabernacle; otherwise a profound bow.

### PART A — PRAYERS AT THE FOOT OF THE ALTAR (Mass of the Catechumens begins)

**1. Entrance / Approach.** Priest (+ servers; at Solemn Mass + deacon, subdeacon, MC, thurifer, acolytes) processes in. At Low/Cantata the priest carries the veiled chalice and places it at the center of the altar; **at Solemn Mass the chalice is prepared beforehand on the credence table.** Priest hands biretta to server (a 1962-specific point: pre-1962 he approached *capite cooperto*). Genuflects/bows at the foot. *Congregation: stand.*

**2. Sign of the Cross + Psalm 42 (Judica me).** Priest at the foot makes the Sign of the Cross, then recites **Psalm 42 antiphonally** with the server(s) (Solemn: with deacon & subdeacon). · *Spoken; at Low Mass audibly to the server; at sung Mass said quietly while the choir sings the Introit.* · *Congregation: kneel (Low) / stand then kneel (High).*
  - **BRANCH — Psalm 42 OMITTED** in Passiontide ferias (Masses of the season) and in **Requiems**. There, after "Introibo… Adjutorium nostrum," go straight to the Confiteor.

**3. Confiteor.** Priest bows profoundly, says the Confiteor; strikes breast 3× at "mea culpa." Server(s) respond *Misereatur*, then say their Confiteor; priest says *Misereatur* + *Indulgentiam* (Sign of the Cross). · *Spoken.* · *Kneel.*
  - **Solemn Mass:** the **deacon and subdeacon** make the responses; the deacon sings the Confiteor at Solemn Masses (recited by servers at Sung/Low).

**4. Versicles + Aufer a nobis + Oramus te.** Priest says "Deus tu conversus…," then ascends to the altar saying *Aufer a nobis* (silent), bows and says *Oramus te* (silent), then **kisses the altar** in the middle (venerating the relics). · *Silent.* · *Kneel.*

### PART B — INTROIT THROUGH COLLECT

**5. Introit.** Priest goes to the **Epistle side**, makes the Sign of the Cross, reads the Introit antiphon + psalm verse + Gloria Patri + antiphon. · *Low: spoken aloud. Sung Mass: choir sings the Introit; priest reads it quietly.* · *Kneel (Low) / sit or stand per custom.*
  - **Solemn/Sung incensation of the altar** here (after the prayers at the foot): priest imposes & blesses incense, incenses the cross (3 double swings), relics, and the whole altar with single swings; then **the celebrant is incensed** by the deacon with 3 double swings. The Missal is removed during the incensing of the altar.
  - **Requiem branch:** at the Introit the priest makes the Sign of the Cross **over the Missal** (as if blessing), not on himself; the Gloria Patri of the Introit is replaced by "Requiem aeternam."

**6. Kyrie.** Priest returns to **center**; says/sings the Kyrie (9 invocations) alternating with server/ministers. · *Low: spoken. Sung: choir sings; priest recites quietly, then may sit at the sedilia if the setting is long.* · *Kneel (Low) / stand, sit when priest sits (High).*

**7. Gloria.** **BRANCH (said or omitted — see Axis 2).** If said: priest at center intones "Gloria in excelsis Deo" (bows at *Deo*), extends/raises hands, continues. · *Low: spoken aloud. Sung: priest intones, choir continues from "Et in terra pax"; priest reads the rest quietly, then sits at the sedilia until the choir finishes.* · *Stand (sit when priest sits at High Mass).* · At High Mass the deacon & subdeacon flank the celebrant.

**8. Dominus vobiscum + Collect(s).** Priest kisses the altar, turns by the Epistle side: "Dominus vobiscum" (R. *Et cum spiritu tuo*); turns back, goes to the Missal (Epistle side), "Oremus," reads the **Collect** (hands extended; bows head at the Holy Name). · *Aloud / sung.* · *Stand.*
  - **BRANCH — Commemorations:** additional Collects follow the day's Collect.
  - **Bishop:** says "Pax vobis" instead of "Dominus vobiscum" after the Gloria.

### PART C — EPISTLE THROUGH GOSPEL

**9. Epistle.** **Low/Cantata:** priest reads (may sing) at the Epistle side; server responds "Deo gratias." **Solemn:** the **subdeacon** chants the Epistle. · *Spoken (Low) / sung (Solemn & often Cantata).* · *Sit.*

**10. Gradual / Alleluia / Tract / (Sequence).** **BRANCH (see Axis 2).** Priest reads them quietly at the Epistle side; choir sings. · *Sit.* · On certain Lenten Tracts and at the *Dies Irae* there are prescribed kneelings/genuflections.

**11. Munda cor meum + preparation for the Gospel.** Priest goes to **center**, bows profoundly, says *Munda cor meum* (silent). At Low Mass the Missal is carried to the **Gospel side** and set diagonally. · *Silent.* · *Sit → prepare to stand.*
  - **Solemn Mass Gospel choreography:** Priest imposes & blesses incense at center. The **deacon** kneels, says *Munda cor meum*, takes the Book of Gospels, kneels for the celebrant's blessing, kisses his hand. **Procession to the Gospel (north) side:** thurifer leads, then two **acolytes with candles**, then the deacon (carrying the Evangeliarium) with the **subdeacon at his left**. The **subdeacon holds the book** (between the two acolytes' candles); the deacon **incenses the book** (3 swings: center, right, left) before chanting; the **celebrant stands at the Epistle side** and listens.
  - **Requiem branch:** acolytes carry **no candles** at the Gospel and **no incense** is used (two acolytes without candles hold the book); only *Munda cor meum* is said (the *Jube Domine benedicere / Dominus sit in corde meo* blessing is omitted).

**12. Gospel.** Priest (Low) or **deacon** (Solemn) sings/reads. "Dominus vobiscum," "Sequentia sancti Evangelii secundum N.," signs the book + forehead/lips/breast (all do likewise). Reads/chants the Gospel; genuflects/bows toward the book at the appropriate words; bows head at the Holy Name. End: server "Laus tibi, Christe." · *Aloud (Low) / sung (Solemn & often Cantata).* · **Congregation: STAND** (the one place all stand at Low Mass).
  - **End of Gospel:** Priest kisses the book: "Per evangelica dicta…"
  - **Solemn:** subdeacon carries the book to the celebrant to kiss; **deacon incenses the celebrant** (3 double swings).
  - **Requiem branch:** the book is **not kissed** and "Per evangelica dicta" is **not said.**

**13. Homily / Sermon (if any).** Priest at the pulpit; may read Epistle & Gospel in the vernacular first; removes maniple. · *Vernacular, spoken.* · *Sit.*

**14. Credo.** **BRANCH (said or omitted — see Axis 2).** If said: priest at center intones "Credo in unum Deum," extends/raises/rejoins hands, bowing. · *Low: spoken. Sung: priest intones, choir continues "Patrem omnipotentem"; priest reads quietly, then sits at the sedilia until the choir finishes.* · *Stand; sit when priest sits (High).*
  - **GENUFLECTION at "Et incarnatus est … et homo factus est":** priest (and all) genuflect. **At Solemn/Sung Mass:** ministers genuflect when the words are *recited*, and **kneel when they are sung** by the choir (kneeling even at the sedilia). · *Congregation kneels at "Et incarnatus."*
  - Sign of the Cross at "et vitam venturi saeculi. Amen." This marks the end of the **Mass of the Catechumens.**

### PART D — OFFERTORY (Mass of the Faithful begins)

**15. Offertory antiphon.** Priest kisses the altar, "Dominus vobiscum," "Oremus," reads the **Offertory antiphon** at the Missal. · *Aloud; at sung Mass the choir sings it, priest reads quietly.* · *Sit.*

**16. Offering of the Host + Wine.** Priest uncovers the chalice; offers the host on the paten (*Suscipe sancte Pater*, silent), makes the Sign of the Cross with the paten, slides the host onto the corporal. Goes to the Epistle side, pours wine into the chalice; blesses & adds a drop of water (*Deus qui humanae substantiae*; water **not blessed** in Requiems); returns to center, offers the chalice (*Offerimus tibi*, silent), covers with the pall. *In spiritu humilitatis; Veni Sanctificator* (Sign of the Cross over the gifts). · *Silent (the medieval Offertory prayers are said secreto).* · *Sit.*
  - **Solemn Mass:** the **subdeacon** (wearing the **humeral veil**) brings the chalice/paten to the altar; the **deacon** pours the wine and presents the host to the celebrant; the subdeacon presents the water for blessing. After the offering, the subdeacon **holds the paten, veiled in the humeral veil, from the Offertory until after the Pater Noster**, standing behind the celebrant. (Not done in Requiems.)

**17. Incensation of the gifts (Solemn & Cantata with incense).** Priest imposes & blesses incense; incenses the gifts (3× crosswise over chalice/host, 3× around), then the cross and altar. Then the **incensation order:** deacon incenses the **celebrant** (3 double swings) → **choir/clergy** → **subdeacon** (holding the paten); then the thurifer incenses the **deacon**, then **MC/acolytes**, then the **people**. · Double-swing rule: **3** for celebrant/choir/people, **2** for prelates/relics/images, **1** for priests in the sanctuary; **single** swings for the altar itself. · *Stand to be incensed, then sit.*

**18. Lavabo.** Priest at the Epistle side washes his fingertips (*Lavabo inter innocentes*, Ps. 25; **Gloria Patri omitted in Requiems & Passiontide**). · *Silent/quiet.* · *Sit.*

**19. Suscipe sancta Trinitas.** Priest at center, bows, says it silently; kisses the altar. · *Silent.* · *Sit.*

**20. Orate fratres + Secret.** Priest kisses the altar, turns to the people (turning back by the **Gospel side** — one of only two times he turns full circle), says "Orate fratres" aloud (rest silent); server responds *Suscipiat*. Then the **Secret** prayer(s) read with hands extended, **NO "Oremus," entirely silent**, ending aloud "Per omnia saecula saeculorum." R. *Amen.* · *Secret = silent (hence the name); conclusion aloud.* · *Sit → kneel toward the end (Low).*
  - **BRANCH:** commemoration Secrets here, paralleling the Collects.

### PART E — PREFACE & CANON

**21. Preface.** "Dominus vobiscum / Sursum corda / Gratias agamus." Priest sings/says the **Preface** (hands extended). · *Aloud / sung — the Preface and the Canon's final doxology are the principal parts of the Mass of the Faithful said ALOUD.* · *Stand (at Low Mass, kneel — see posture note).*
  - **BRANCH — Proper Preface:** the text varies by season/feast (e.g., Trinity Preface on Sundays per annum; Paschal, Christmas, Apostles, Preface of the Dead, etc.). The *slot* is fixed; the text is a Proper.

**22. Sanctus.** Priest bows, joins hands, says the Sanctus (quieter); **bell rung.** Sign of the Cross at "Benedictus qui venit." · *Low: spoken. Sung: choir sings — and because the chant may run into the Canon, the priest does NOT wait but proceeds.* · **Congregation: KNEEL** (from the Sanctus onward; kneeling continues at Low Mass).
  - **Solemn Mass:** at the end of the Preface the **torches** are lit by the acolytes (minimum two; an even number, up to six), carried in, and the bearers kneel through the Canon.

**23. Canon (Te igitur → …).** Priest at center, hands extended; the **Canon is said almost entirely SILENTLY** (secreto) in its fixed, unchanging form. Numerous signs of the cross over the offerings; kisses altar; bows. · *Silent.* · *Kneel.*
  - **BRANCH — proper Communicantes & Hanc igitur:** proper insertions on Christmas, Epiphany, Easter, Ascension, Pentecost (Communicantes) and Easter/Pentecost (Hanc igitur). The bell is rung once at the **Hanc igitur** (hands extended over the chalice).
  - **Requiem branch:** more kneeling; the prayer for peace / Pax later is omitted.

**24. Consecration & Elevations.** *Qui pridie* — priest takes the host, bows over the altar, says the words of consecration in a low voice: **HOC EST ENIM CORPUS MEUM.** Genuflects, **elevates the Host**, genuflects (bell rung 3×; at Solemn Mass the **thurifer incenses the Host and Chalice** at each elevation, 3 swings each). Then consecrates the chalice (*Simili modo… HIC EST ENIM CALIX…*), genuflects, **elevates the Chalice**, genuflects. From here the priest keeps thumb & forefinger joined until the ablutions. · *Words of consecration said silently/low.* · **Congregation: KNEEL, look up at each elevation.** At Solemn Mass the deacon lifts the chasuble, uncovers/covers the chalice with the pall, and kneels at the celebrant's side.

**25. Anamnesis → end of Canon.** *Unde et memores, Supra quae, Supplices, Memento* (dead), *Nobis quoque* (priest raises voice slightly for "Nobis quoque peccatoribus," strikes breast once), *Per quem haec omnia.* · *Silent.* · *Kneel.*

**26. Minor Elevation + doxology.** *Per ipsum…*: priest makes Signs of the Cross with the Host over the chalice, slightly elevates Host & chalice together ("omnis honor et gloria"). Concludes **aloud:** "Per omnia saecula saeculorum." R. *Amen.* · *Doxology conclusion aloud.* · *Kneel.* (At Solemn Mass a single bell signals the minor elevation; the subdeacon still holds the paten.)

### PART F — COMMUNION RITE

**27. Pater Noster.** "Oremus / Praeceptis salutaribus moniti…" Priest sings/says the **Our Father aloud** (hands extended); server/choir responds "Sed libera nos a malo." · *Aloud / sung — the priest says/sings the whole prayer (unlike the Novus Ordo).* · *Kneel.*

**28. Libera nos / Fraction / Commixtio.** Priest takes the paten (at Solemn Mass the **subdeacon now returns the paten** to the deacon and **removes the humeral veil**), says *Libera nos* (silent), breaks the Host into three, "Pax Domini sit semper vobiscum" (R. *Et cum spiritu tuo*) — making Signs of the Cross with the particle — drops the particle into the chalice (*Haec commixtio*, silent). · *Mostly silent; "Pax Domini" aloud.* · *Kneel.*
  - **Pax / Kiss of Peace — Solemn Mass only:** during the Agnus Dei the celebrant gives the **Pax** (a light embrace) to the deacon, who passes it to the subdeacon, thence to clergy in choir. · **OMITTED in Requiems and on Maundy Thursday.** Not given at Low or Cantata.

**29. Agnus Dei.** Priest bows, says/sings the Agnus Dei (strikes breast 3×). · *Low: spoken. Sung: choir sings.* · *Kneel.*
  - **Requiem branch:** "dona eis requiem" / "…sempiternam" replaces "miserere nobis / dona nobis pacem"; the **breast is not struck.**

**30. Prayers before Communion + Priest's Communion.** Priest says the 3 prayers (*Domine Jesu Christe qui dixisti; Domine Jesu Christe Fili Dei vivi; Perceptio Corporis tui* — silent). "Panem caelestem accipiam"; "Domine non sum dignus" 3× (struck breast, voice raised slightly). Consumes the **Host**, then (*Quid retribuam*) the **Precious Blood.** · *Silent except the triple Domine non sum dignus.* · *Kneel.*

**31. Communion of the Faithful (if any).** Priest genuflects, takes the ciborium, turns: "Ecce Agnus Dei… Domine non sum dignus" (3×). Distributes on the tongue, the communicant kneeling at the rail: "Corpus Domini nostri Jesu Christi custodiat animam tuam…" (priest says the entire formula including Amen; the communicant makes no response). · *Spoken.* · *Communicants kneel; receive on the tongue.*
  - **1962 BRANCH:** the **server's/deacon's Confiteor before the people's Communion is OMITTED** in 1962 (it was said pre-1962). The *Ecce Agnus Dei* + triple *Domine non sum dignus* are still said. Note: the Confiteor was "omitted, not expressly forbidden," and survives in the 1962 Pontificale and on Good Friday — hence some communities retain it.

**32. Ablutions.** Priest purifies the chalice with wine, then with wine & water over his fingers (*Quod ore sumpsimus; Corpus tuum*), at the Epistle side; dries and reassembles the chalice, veils it. · *Silent.* · *Sit.*

### PART G — CONCLUSION

**33. Communion antiphon + Postcommunion.** Priest reads the **Communion antiphon** at the Missal (Epistle side; choir sings at sung Mass). Returns to center, kisses the altar, "Dominus vobiscum," goes to the Missal, "Oremus," reads the **Postcommunion** prayer(s). · *Aloud / sung.* · *Stand.*
  - **BRANCH:** commemoration Postcommunions here.

**34. Ite Missa est / Benedicamus Domino / Requiescant in pace.** Priest (deacon at Solemn Mass) kisses the altar, turns: "Dominus vobiscum," then the **dismissal**. · *Aloud / sung.* · *Stand (kneel for the blessing).*
  - **BRANCH (see Axis 2):** **Ite Missa est** if the Gloria was said; **Benedicamus Domino** if the Gloria was omitted; **Requiescant in pace** at Requiems (R. *Amen*). "Alleluia, alleluia" added in the Easter octave & Pentecost.

**35. Placeat + Blessing.** Priest bows, says *Placeat tibi sancta Trinitas* (silent), kisses the altar, raises his eyes, gives the **blessing** ("Benedicat vos omnipotens Deus…") with the Sign of the Cross. · *Blessing aloud.* · **Congregation: KNEEL.**
  - **Requiem branch:** **NO blessing** (the Placeat is said, but the final blessing — and at Solemn Mass the blessing of deacon/subdeacon — is omitted).

**36. Last Gospel.** Priest goes to the **Gospel side**, "Dominus vobiscum," "Initium sancti Evangelii secundum Joannem," reads the **Prologue of St. John (John 1:1–14).** **GENUFLECTION at "Et Verbum caro factum est."** End: "Deo gratias." · *Aloud (spoken even at sung Mass).* · **Congregation: stand; genuflect/kneel at "Et Verbum caro factum est."**
  - **BRANCH — Proper Last Gospel:** On certain days a different ("proper") Last Gospel replaces John's Prologue — e.g., when a feast displaces a Sunday/privileged feria that is commemorated, the displaced day's Gospel becomes the Last Gospel; on the 3rd Mass of Christmas (whose own Gospel *is* John's Prologue) the Epiphany Gospel is used. The Ordo flags these.
  - **Requiem branch:** the Last Gospel is St. John's Prologue normally, BUT it is **omitted when the Absolution at the catafalque follows immediately** (1960 Code of Rubrics no. 510e; the *Ordo Absolutionis* of the 1962 Missal specifies omission whether or not the body is present).

### PART H — AFTER MASS

**37a. Leonine Prayers (after Low Mass ONLY).** Priest kneels at the foot of the altar and leads, in the vernacular: 3 Hail Marys, Salve Regina + versicle/collect, Prayer to St. Michael, and (by Pius X's 1904 permission) the threefold "Most Sacred Heart of Jesus, have mercy on us" (*Cor Jesu sacratissimum, miserere nobis*), "a permission that was universally availed of." · *Spoken, vernacular.* · *Kneel.*
  - **OMITTED after:** all sung/Solemn Masses; Requiem/funeral Masses; conventual Masses; Masses immediately followed by another function (Benediction, procession); Masses with external solemnity (weddings, ordinations, First Communions, Confirmations); the First Friday/Thursday/Saturday privileged votive Masses.
  - **Status:** Officially suppressed by the Sacred Congregation of Rites' Instruction *Inter Oecumenici* (26 Sept. 1964, effective 7 March 1965), §48.j: "The last gospel is omitted; the Leonine Prayers are suppressed." Because they were never printed in the Missal and the suppression postdates 1962, most communities retain them after Low Mass under *Summorum Pontificum*.

**37b. Absolution at the Catafalque (after a Requiem, when applicable).** After a funeral Mass (obligatory) or other Requiem (optional), the celebrant changes the chasuble/maniple for a **black cope** and processes to the coffin/catafalque. He sings *Non intres in judicium* (funeral) → Responsory **Libera me, Domine** → Kyrie → Pater Noster (silent) while **sprinkling (holy water) and incensing the catafalque** → versicles → concluding prayer (*Deus cui proprium* for a named person; *Absolve quaesumus* when the body is absent). For a funeral with body present, the procession to burial follows with *In paradisum*. The Absolution must be given by the celebrant of the Mass (only the diocesan bishop may absolve after a Mass he did not celebrate). · *Sung/spoken.* · *Kneel/stand as directed.*

## CONGREGATIONAL POSTURE SUMMARY
(Postures of the laity were never strictly codified by the rubrics — these are the customary patterns; the 1960 reform encouraged adapting the sung-Mass postures to Low Mass too.)

**Low Mass:** Kneel for most of the Mass; stand only for the Gospel (and the Last Gospel). Common variants: stand at the Gloria/Credo with the priest, sit for the Epistle and sermon, kneel from the Sanctus through Communion. Many simply kneel throughout except the Gospels. Kneel for the Leonine Prayers.

**High / Sung / Solemn Mass:** Follow the sacred ministers (the "clergy in choir" model). Stand for the entrance/Asperges, Gloria (sit when the priest sits), Collect, Gospel, Credo (genuflect at *Et incarnatus*, sit when the priest sits while the choir sings), and Preface; **kneel from the Sanctus through Communion** (a known point of variation: some remain standing for the sung Sanctus until the consecration bell, following the older "clergy in choir" custom). Sit for the Epistle, Gradual, Offertory, and whenever the priest is seated. Stand for the Postcommunion and the dismissal/Last Gospel; kneel for the blessing.

**Requiem:** More kneeling overall (e.g., for the Collect and the conclusion of the Canon); some charts have the congregation stand for the *Dies Irae*; kneel/stand at the Absolution as directed.

## QUICK BRANCH-FACTOR INDEX (flowchart logic)

- **Principal Sunday Mass?** → Asperges (Vidi Aquam in Paschaltide) precedes; omit on Palm Sunday / Pontifical / Exposition.
- **Form?** → Low (spoken, servers, Leonine Prayers after) / Cantata (sung, choir, incense optional, no deacon) / Solemn (deacon + subdeacon, full incense, Pax, sit at sedilia).
- **Gloria?** → by season/rank/color (omit if violet / Requiem / most IV-class).
- **Credo?** → by rank (Sundays + I-class + certain II-class + octaves + I-class votives).
- **Epistle→Gospel chant?** → Gradual+Alleluia (default) / Gradual+Tract (Septuagesima–Lent) / double Alleluia (Paschaltide) / + Sequence (the 5 days).
- **Requiem?** → black; no Psalm 42, no Gloria, no Credo, no Gloria Patri (Introit & Lavabo), Dies Irae (*ad lib* at daily), no kiss/"Per evangelica dicta" at Gospel, water not blessed, no Pax, Agnus "dona eis requiem," no blessing, Last Gospel omitted if Absolution follows, Absolution at the catafalque, Requiescant in pace, no Leonine Prayers.
- **Dismissal?** → Ite Missa est (Gloria said) / Benedicamus Domino (Gloria omitted) / Requiescant in pace (Requiem).
- **Last Gospel?** → John's Prologue (default) / proper Last Gospel (commemorated displaced day) / omitted (Requiem + Absolution).
- **After Mass?** → Leonine Prayers (Low Mass only).

## TL;DR
- The 1962 Mass has **one fixed skeleton** (Prayers at the Foot → Mass of the Catechumens: Introit, Kyrie, Gloria, Collect, Epistle, Gradual/Alleluia/Tract/Sequence, Gospel, Credo → Mass of the Faithful: Offertory, Secret, Preface, Canon/Consecration, Pater Noster, Agnus Dei, Communion, Postcommunion, dismissal, blessing, Last Gospel), onto which three "axes" of branching are layered: **form/solemnity, the liturgical day & season, and the preliminary Asperges.**
- **Form** changes *who* does each part and *how* it is rendered — Low Mass (priest + server, spoken, Leonine Prayers after) → Missa Cantata (sung by priest & choir, incense optional, no deacon) → Solemn Mass (deacon chants Gospel, subdeacon chants Epistle and holds the veiled paten, full incensation order, Kiss of Peace, priest sits at the sedilia while the choir finishes long chants).
- **Day/season** toggles discrete switches — Gloria on/off, Credo on/off, Gradual+Alleluia vs. Gradual+Tract vs. double Alleluia (± one of five Sequences), liturgical color, dismissal form, commemorations, and the whole **Requiem** variant (black, no Gloria/Credo/Pax/blessing, Dies Irae, Absolution at the catafalque); the **weekday name itself changes nothing** — only the calendar's rank and season do.

## Key Findings
- **The Ordinary is invariant; the variability is structured.** A faithful interactive page should render the master sequence once and attach conditional flags at each node (Gloria? Credo? which inter-lesson chant? which dismissal? Requiem? proper Last Gospel?). The branch logic above maps cleanly to boolean/enumerated toggles.
- **"Silent vs. aloud" and "spoken vs. sung" are first-class attributes of each part**, and they shift by form. The Canon is silent in all forms except its framing aloud ("Per omnia…", Preface, Pater Noster, doxology conclusion). At sung Masses the celebrant reads quietly what the choir sings — the defining "silent-while-sung" overlap, with the priest sitting at the sedilia during long Gloria/Credo settings.
- **Physical geography is rule-bound:** Epistle side (south) vs. Gospel side (north) vs. center; the priest turns and returns by the Epistle side except at the *Orate fratres* and final blessing; genuflections/kisses of the altar punctuate transitions; the two fixed genuflections of the people are at *Et incarnatus* (Creed) and *Et Verbum caro factum est* (Last Gospel).
- **The Requiem is the single largest coherent branch** and is best modeled as a distinct "mode" that disables many nodes (Judica, Gloria, Credo, Gloria Patri instances, Pax, blessing, the Gospel kiss) and enables others (Dies Irae, Absolution at the catafalque, *Requiescant in pace*).
- **1962-specific deltas** to surface for accuracy: suppressed pre-Communion Confiteor; Ite Missa est largely displacing Benedicamus Domino; incense permitted at every Cantata; biretta handed off at the foot. These are the points most often gotten wrong by general references.

## Recommendations (for building the interactive page)
1. **Model the Mass as a single ordered node list** (steps 1–37) with per-node metadata fields: `actor`, `position`, `action`, `voice` (silent/aloud), `mode` (spoken/sung), `posture_low`, `posture_high`, and `branch_flags`. This directly mirrors the structure above.
2. **Implement the three axes as global toggles** the user sets first: *Form* (Low / Cantata / Solemn), *Day-type* (build from: season, class, feast-vs-feria, color, Gloria-bool, Credo-bool, inter-lesson-chant-enum, Sequence-enum, dismissal-enum), and *Asperges* (none / Asperges / Vidi Aquam). Render only the relevant variant text/notes per node.
3. **Treat "Requiem" as a master switch** that cascades the disable/enable set listed in the Branch-Factor Index, rather than as many independent toggles — this prevents inconsistent states.
4. **Do not hard-code Propers.** At the Introit, Collect, Epistle, Gradual block, Gospel, Offertory, Secret, Preface, Communion, and Postcommunion nodes, insert a labeled "Proper slot — determined by the calendar (see the day's Missal/Ordo)" placeholder, exactly as the user requested, with a link-out for the user's own text fetching.
5. **Flag the genuine variation points** (so the page is honest where practice differs): congregational posture for the sung Sanctus (stand-until-bell vs. kneel-from-Sanctus); retention of the pre-Communion Confiteor (omitted in 1962 rubrics but practiced by some); whether the celebrant is incensed after the Gospel at a Cantata; and the deacon's Gospel orientation ("facing the people" per the 1962 Ritus servandus vs. the traditional "north" geography). **Thresholds to revisit content:** if the page later targets pre-1955 usage, the Holy Week, octaves, Benedicamus distribution, and pre-Communion Confiteor sections must be rebuilt.
6. **Cite authoritative sources in the page's "about" panel:** the 1962 *Ritus servandus in celebratione Missae* (printed in the Missal; the cited section numbers VI §5, VII §9–10, VIII §8, X §8 are reliable anchors), the General Rubrics (nn. 269–530), and Fortescue–O'Connell–Reid, *The Ceremonies of the Roman Rite Described* (15th ed., 2009), the standard ceremonial manual; supplement with FSSP/ICKSP/Una Voce/Latin Mass Society guides for practical postures.

## Caveats
- **Lay postures were never comprehensively codified** by the rubrics (they govern the clergy). The posture tables here reflect widespread custom and the 1960-era adaptation of sung-Mass postures to Low Mass; local custom legitimately varies, and the page should say so.
- **The 1962 *Ritus servandus* says the deacon at the Gospel faces "the people"** while older manuals (and Fortescue's geography) describe him facing **liturgical north**; in an *ad orientem* sanctuary these largely coincide. Flagged as a real point of descriptive divergence.
- **The pre-Communion Confiteor** is **omitted in the 1962 Ritus servandus but not expressly forbidden**, and it persists in the 1962 Pontificale and on Good Friday — so some FSSP/SSPX-adjacent communities still do it. Present it as "omitted in 1962; sometimes retained."
- **Leonine Prayers** were formally suppressed in 1965 (*Inter Oecumenici* §48.j), after the 1962 books; their post-Low-Mass use today rests on the fact that they were never in the Missal and on the *Summorum Pontificum* framework. Treat as customary, not strictly rubrical.
- Several supporting details were corroborated from secondary explanatory sites (Wikipedia, New Liturgical Movement, parish/diocesan guides, FIUV position papers) rather than the Latin typical edition directly; the core flow, the *Ritus servandus* section anchors, and the Fortescue ceremonial points were cross-checked, but for publication-grade certainty on any contested rubric the editor should verify against a physical 1962 altar Missal and Fortescue–O'Connell–Reid.
- **Numbers/limits that vary by source or custom:** number of torchbearers (minimum two; commonly an even number up to six), number of Cantata candles (four or six), and the exact set of days on which the Leonine Prayers are omitted (handbooks list slightly different cases). These are noted as ranges rather than single values.
