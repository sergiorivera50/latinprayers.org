latinprayers.org

Manifesto

In the defense of Tradition, the Tridentine Mass, and Catholic living.

latinprayers.org exists for one purpose: to set before you the prayers of the Church in the tongue she has always prayed them, and to commend the Tradition from which they came. What follows is not opinion offered for debate, but the conviction on which this work stands.

I

On Tradition

The Catholic faith is received, not invented. It was handed down from the Apostles, guarded by the Fathers, and defined by the councils — and it is ours to hand on whole, not to revise to the temper of the age. What was sacred and true for nineteen centuries remains sacred and true now.

II

On the Mass

We honour it not as a relic but as the Church's highest prayer, and we hold that the faithful are right to seek it, to love it, and to ask that it be restored to its rightful place.

III

On Catholic Living

The faith is not confined to the hour of worship. It orders the home, the family, and the commonwealth toward their proper end — the reign of Christ the King over every heart and every nation. To live as a Catholic is to order all things, private and public, to Him.
